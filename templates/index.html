Project Overview

Heart disease remains one of the leading causes of mortality worldwide, and traditional medical records show lifestyle changes, dietary habits, and lack of physical activity. Despite advances in medicine, prevention and early detection are crucial in reducing the risk of cardiovascular diseases. Analyzing large-scale health data related to heart disease—such as patient demographics, medical history, lifestyle choices, and clinical measures—requires advanced tools for extracting insights.

In this project, we choose target labels to detect cardiac risk indicators and build predictive models that analyze heart disease factors. The goal is to develop a user-friendly, interactive dashboard that highlights key risk factors, and identify trends that can support clinical decision-making. By leveraging database storage and modern web analytics, users can visualize data over time, compare patient groups, and test hypotheses through dashboard components.

User Personas & Scenarios
Dr. Sharma
Clinical Specialist
Dr. Sharma is a senior cardiologist working in a metropolitan hospital. She notices an increasing number of patients coming with heart issues, and wants an interactive dashboard highlighting key risk factors. She needs to identify trends rapidly to support clinical decisions.
Ramesh
Data Analyst
Ramesh is a health data analyst investigating correlations between demographics (age, gender) and clinical indicators like cholesterol or vessel count. He wants to query the patient database and export reports comparing different profiles.
Anya
Self-Assessment
Anya is a 38-year old woman with a family history of heart disease. She wants to check her own cardiac health risk factors (resting blood pressure, ECG, cholesterol) and get immediate diagnostic estimates alongside healthy lifestyle recommendations.
